Overview of Population, Housing Market Trends, and Average Income in Malaysia (2023)

The scatterplot visualises the relationship between populations and housing transactions for each states in Malaysia. The calculated R Squared value is 0.365584789 (calculated in Excel) where it shows a weak relationship between housing transactions and population for each states in Malaysia while only 36.6% of the variance in housing transactions can be explained by the population. We can assume that the relationship between this 2 factors may due to other factors. Each point represents a single state, with population and housing markets.

The stacked bar chart depicts the comparison between average salaries and houing markets across different states in Malaysia. With high income states, Kuala Lumpur but have lower housing transactions probably due to market saturation or etc. and mid-income states like Johor and Perak remain vibrant housing markets and ongoing development projects.

The donut chart visualises the population share of each states in Malaysia, which shows an intuitive view of how people are distributed across Malaysia. From the chart, Selangor clearly dominates the chart as the Malaysia's most populous state due to the extensive urbanisation, job opportunities and infrastructure and Putrajaya has the lowest population as it mainly to host government ministries.
